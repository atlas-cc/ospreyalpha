§ 03 · The Architecture

What makes it an operating system rather than a smart assistant comes down to four things working together.

Spec. 01

Memory that compounds.

Permanent · searchable · shared

Every conversation it has ever had is permanently searchable. Durable facts load into every interaction, and a shared semantic mesh means knowledge learned in one corner of the business is available everywhere. It never starts from zero.

Spec. 02

Learns from being corrected.

Once is enough

Tell it it's wrong once and that correction is written to memory at high priority. It doesn't make that mistake again. Over weeks it gets sharper, not stale.

Spec. 03

Commands a team, not just itself.

An AI org chart, one brain on top

Osprey sits on top of a fleet of specialized agents: one running inbound leads, one for each brand in the portfolio, an executive assistant running the founder's calendar. It coordinates all of them.

Spec. 04

Operates with discipline.

Verified · scoped · auditable

It can't confidently lie. It is forced to verify before claiming something's broken or stating a number. It stays in its lane, never bleeds one brand's data into another, and does exactly what's asked instead of freelancing. That's what makes it safe enough to hand the keys to.

§ 07 · Security

Your business. Your data. Your rules.

The first question every owner asks is the right one. Here is exactly how Osprey is kept private, safe, and under your control.

01 · Private by Default

Your own server. Nobody else's.

Your Osprey runs single-tenant, on infrastructure dedicated to your company alone. Your data is never shared, never pooled, and never used to train anyone else's AI.

02 · Encrypted Everywhere

AES-256, at rest and in transit.

Bank-grade encryption on everything, behind firewalled infrastructure. Your credentials stay in a sealed vault that even the agents can only use, never read out.

03 · You Approve What Matters

Nothing irreversible without you.

Routine work runs on its own. Payments, refunds, and anything customer-facing you flag waits in a queue for your one-tap approval. You write the rules once. Osprey follows them.

04 · Every Action Logged

A full audit trail. Always.

Every single thing Osprey does is logged, timestamped, and replayable. You can see exactly what happened, when, and why. No black box.

05 · Brands Stay Separated

Hard walls between entities.

If you run more than one company, each one is isolated. One brand's data, customers, and numbers never bleed into another's.

06 · One-Click Exit

No lock-in. Ever.

Export everything any time, or wipe it clean. The software we build during your deployment is yours and stays yours. Leave whenever you want.

§ 11 · FAQ

Questions every owner asks. Straight answers.

Is this just ChatGPT with extra steps?
No. ChatGPT waits for you to type a question, gives you an answer, and forgets you the moment you close the tab. Osprey is connected to your actual business: your store, your inbox, your books, your ad accounts. It works on its own schedule, remembers everything permanently, and completes the work instead of describing it. You read a morning brief, not a chat window.
What exactly is an "agent"?
A focused AI worker with one job, its own instructions, and hard limits on what it can access. One watches revenue. One handles the inbox and calendar. One watches your infrastructure. Osprey is the manager on top that briefs them, checks their work, and reports to you. You only ever deal with Osprey.
Will it do things without asking me?
Only the routine things you've approved it to do: reports, reminders, monitoring, drafts. Anything irreversible waits for you. Payments, refunds, and customer-facing sends you flag sit in a queue until you approve them with one tap. You set those rules once during setup, and you can change them any time.
Is my data safe?
Your Osprey runs on its own private, single-tenant server. AES-256 encryption, firewalled, full audit trail. Your data is never shared, never pooled with other customers, and never used to train anyone else's AI. If you ever leave, one click exports everything or wipes it clean.
What do I actually do day to day?
Three things. Read the morning brief (about 90 seconds). Tap the approvals that need your judgment (usually one to three a day). Correct it when it's wrong, once, and it never makes that mistake again. That's the whole job. Everything else is Osprey's problem now.
What happens when it gets something wrong?
Two safety nets. Before acting, Osprey is forced to verify: it can't claim something is broken or state a number without checking first. After acting, every action is logged and replayable, so nothing is a mystery. And when you correct it, that correction is written to memory at high priority. One correction, permanent fix.
Do I need to be technical?
No. We do the entire installation. A senior partner embeds with you for two weeks, connects your tools, writes your rules, and configures everything to how you already run the business. You never touch a line of code. If you can read a text message and tap a button, you can run Osprey.
How long until it's actually running my operations?
Useful in week one, running daily by week eight. The first two weeks are recon: we embed and map your business. Weeks three to eight we build and configure your Osprey. Then it runs every day with us alongside for the first quarter. By month four, your team owns it and we stay on call.
What does it replace, and what does it not?
It absorbs the work you'd otherwise hire for: an ops manager, a reporting analyst, a deployment engineer, a coordinator, an EA. Roughly $540K a year of operational surface area across a typical portfolio. It does not replace your judgment. Decisions stay with you; the grind moves to Osprey.
